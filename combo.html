01 — What I built

Analytics

KPI Tracker

Outreach and BD analytics dashboard

The problem

BD was spread across Telegram, X, email, and calls with no single source of truth. The team couldn't tell which channels were working or where deals were quietly leaking.

What I built

A dashboard pulling outreach, reply rates, sentiment, funnel health, and a follow-up cadence calendar into one place — three views: Overview, Follow-ups, Channels & Messages.

The impact

Open it, see the best channel, the biggest funnel leak, and exactly who to follow up with that day. Guesswork became clear decisions.

Content Ops

DraftBird

Content approval platform

The problem

Content kept shipping late. Every post needed a founder's sign-off, and approvals were scattered across DMs and spreadsheets.

What I built

A content approval app bringing drafting, urgent flags, review, approvals, scheduling, and analytics into one shared queue.

The impact

Content review went from an hour to an hour and a half every day, down to roughly an hour a week. Same approvals, a fraction of the time.

AI Tooling

DeFi Lead Scanner

AI-powered lead qualifier

The problem

Qualifying which protocols to approach took hours of manual research every week, and a lot of outreach still went to poor-fit leads.

What I built

A human-in-the-loop AI tool that scans DeFi protocols and scores each lead hot, warm, or cold by TVL, fees, and fit, then routes them to a review queue where a person makes the final call.

The impact

Manual research dropped by 70 to 80%. Daily outreach effort down by around 30%. The team stopped digging and spent its time only on leads that actually fit.

Products I've Helped Improve

A collection of products where I contributed through product analysis, UX reviews, onboarding optimization, growth recommendations, and user journey improvements.

I wasn't the founder, designer, or developer of these products. My role was to identify friction, understand user behavior, uncover growth opportunities, and recommend improvements that could improve activation, trust, conversion, and retention.

Context

Reviewed their onboarding flow internally at Refine Studio — QA, feature recommendations, and simplifying the first-time user experience for a DeFi lending protocol.

What I flagged
  • Too many steps before users could do anything useful
  • Value wasn't surfaced early enough to hold new users' attention
  • Key actions were buried with no clear path to them
My Recommendations
  • Add a progress indicator — users need to know how close they are to the end
  • Surface the top APY opportunity on the first screen, not buried in the product
  • Cut the steps to make a first deposit to under three clicks

Context

Reviewed the interface for their multi-asset swap product while at Refine Studio — focused on the first-use experience and where new users were getting lost.

What I flagged
  • No obvious starting point — interface assumed familiarity users didn't have
  • Users had to figure out the UX pattern before they could see any value
  • A simple guided first-swap flow would have done a lot of the work
My Recommendations
  • A "Start your first swap" wizard — guided, not free-form, for first-timers
  • Show expected output before users confirm, not after
  • Surface 3–5 popular trading pairs upfront so users aren't starting from zero

Context

Reviewed how they were handling trust signals for an AI agent that autonomously trades on your behalf — a product where trust is the entire conversion problem.

What I flagged
  • Users had zero visibility into what the AI was doing or why
  • No way to audit past decisions, which makes confidence-building impossible
  • Trust is the conversion driver for anything touching funds — it needed to be built into the UX
My Recommendations
  • Add a decision log: every trade should have a one-line "why" explanation
  • Let users set a risk cap and visibly reflect it in the AI's behavior
  • Show live position rationale in plain language, not just raw P&L numbers

Context

Benchmarked their perpetual DEX against leading competitors including dYdX and Hyperliquid — identifying where the experience gap was largest and which fixes would close it fastest.

What I flagged
  • Gaps vs. dYdX and Hyperliquid were easy to spot — and users would spot them too
