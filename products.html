QAFeature Recommendations
Context

Reviewed onboarding internally at Refine Studio — QA, feature recommendations, and simplifying the first-time user experience for a DeFi lending protocol.

What I flagged
  • Too many steps before users could do anything useful
  • Value wasn't surfaced early enough to hold new users' attention
  • Key actions were buried with no clear path to them
My Recommendations
  • Add a progress indicator — users need to know how close they are to the end
  • Surface the top APY on the first screen, not buried in the product
  • Cut the steps to make a first deposit to under three clicks

UX StrategyUsability Review
Context

Reviewed the multi-asset swap interface at Refine Studio — focused on the first-use experience and where new users were getting lost.

What I flagged
  • No obvious starting point — interface assumed familiarity users didn't have
  • Users had to figure out the UX pattern before they could see any value
  • A guided first-swap flow would have done a lot of the heavy lifting
My Recommendations
  • A "Start your first swap" wizard — guided, not free-form, for first-timers
  • Show expected output before users confirm, not after
  • Surface 3–5 popular trading pairs upfront so users aren't starting from zero

Trust DesignProduct Strategy
Context

Reviewed trust signals for an AI agent that autonomously trades on your behalf — a product where trust is the entire conversion problem.

What I flagged
  • Users had zero visibility into what the AI was doing or why
  • No way to audit past decisions — confidence-building was impossible
  • Trust is the conversion driver for anything touching funds
My Recommendations
  • Add a decision log: every trade should have a one-line "why" explanation
  • Let users set a risk cap and visibly reflect it in the AI's behavior
  • Show live position rationale in plain language, not just raw P&L

UX BenchmarkingMobile UX
Context

Benchmarked their perpetual DEX against dYdX and Hyperliquid — identifying where the experience gap was largest and which fixes would close it fastest.

What I flagged
  • Experience gaps vs. dYdX and Hyperliquid were easy to spot — users would spot them too
  • Mobile friction points top competitors had already solved
  • Order flow was longer than it needed to be
My Recommendations
  • Reduce the open-position flow to two taps on mobile, matching Hyperliquid
  • Add a quick-close button directly on the position card
  • Prioritize the highest-gap UX improvements based on competitor parity analysis

Product GrowthOnboarding Strategy
Context

Worked inside the platform on growth and onboarding strategy — focused on activation, retention, and making the earn loop clear enough to drive repeat participation.

What I flagged
  • Core value loop wasn't clear on first visit — users didn't know what they'd earn
  • Active campaigns were buried, first participation required too much effort
  • Nothing pulling users back after their first campaign
My Recommendations
  • A guided "your first campaign" flow — first participation in under 2 minutes
  • Surface active campaigns on the home screen, not three clicks deep
  • Add a streak or comeback mechanic to create a reason to return next day
